ADP Direct Deposit

Overview

These designs were created as part of my summer 2015 internship with ADP. I worked as a UI / UX Designer and Developer, iterating on the mobile and web designs for a fully responsive direct deposit application. I used a mobile first approach, designing web pages last. The images shown here are of the final iteration I worked on, but I contibuted to about 4 previous iterations of minor UI tweaks before these were settled on. Afterwards, I worked with the development team to implement these designs.

Discovery

As I came on-board once designs were mostly developed, I did little discovery and research. For the most part, my design choices at this point were informed by my own knowledge and skills, as well as the wisdom of my mentors. I worked independently and asked my mentors when necessary for guidance, but did not get a chance in the short time I worked on the project to interview any users about the effectiveness of the designs.

Design

The application begins with a welcome screen, then asks new users to search for their bank. If it is one of the 14 partner banks, it will be listed and have a secure login page. If not, users may choose "Don't see your bank?" and go to the manual bank addition page.

Users can drag and drop their various accounts so that they may appear on the Allocation Summary page. Users may then choose to put any amount of their potential check to be deposited amongst the various accounts, split as they like.





The design is intended to be very straightforward, considering it deals with paychecks, but still powerful enough to allow users to customize their deposits as they desire. The secure partner bank login is intended to remove the need to enter routing and account numbers, which and long and easy to incorrectly type in. Manual account addition catches all banks that do not fit the criteria of the secure login, and is all on one simple page to make the inherently tedious process as painless as possible. Earlier designs had the manual addition page broken into one line of the form per page, with an interesting interaction for changing field types, but was deemed too hassling for the user to be worthwhile.

Finally, informative alert boxes appear throughout, in order to fully inform users of the consequences of their choices. Again, given the nature of the application, these are necessary to help users not become frustrated or confused about such an important topic as their paycheck.
