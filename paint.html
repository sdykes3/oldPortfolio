Paint Chip Simulator

The purpose of this simulator was to create a "drawing" program, but not in the traditional sense. It was also necessary to have the correct feel of whatever medium was chosen.

I chose to do a paint chipping simulator because chipping off paint is fun and has a very unique feel to it. When you scrap paint off, the action is not smooth. It is rough and a little difficult, and when you do manage to get some paint off it often comes unpredictably, in tiny chips or large chunks that remove several layers at once. However, it still feels rewarding to make progress and see what colors lie beneath the surface. Capturing that feeling is the goal of this program.

Roles:
  • Idea and concept
  • Coding and implementation

Development:
  • Individual class project
  • Platform: Processing (open source language and IDE, similar to Java)
