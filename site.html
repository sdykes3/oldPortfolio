Overview

This site has undergone several iterations over the course of my education. Initially, it was merely thrown together in my 2nd year as a means to show my gave development and spriting experience. It was very minimal, without much visual or UX design consideration. As my knowledge and interests expanded throughout the years, and as the needs of my portfolio changed, I changed the design. The site you are using now is the most recent iteration, but likely not the last.

The images to the side show the 3 main iterations. Toward the bottom are the wireframes and limited mockups of the original design idea, which was never developed. The 2nd iteration came a year later and was the result of extensive research into practices of other portfolio websites. I kept the boxes for portfolio items, but added a hover effect to display names only when further inspection was desired, decreasing clutter. Nav bar organization was improved, and visual design was implemented in a way that the original iteration never had. A side banner was added to provide some branding and personality.

The most recent iteration further improves the website. The visual feel of the site has been updated, moving from light and playful colors to a more refined and neutral palette with pops of color. The sidebar was removed to further update the look. Typography improved. Several new home page designs were considered before settling on the current one. The slideshow on the home page was removed and replaced with bold text and a playfully colorful background that channels a bit of my personality. Portfolio items were no longer placed on separate pages by type, but instead filtered for a more cohesive browsing experience. Mobile responsive capabilities were taken into consideration starting with this iteration.

Future tweaks I am making to the site include removal of the about page contact form in favor of email and social media buttons, a process page outlining how I approach design problems, and a new type of layout for formatting iteration examples (such as this). These items are a current work in progress.
